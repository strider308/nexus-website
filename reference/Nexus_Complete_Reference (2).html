Company Brochure

Nexus

We build software for workflows that depend on clear roles, reliable handoffs, and the right information reaching the right person.

The Problem

Important work often does not fail because people are careless. It fails because the process is scattered.

A clinic runs on phone calls, registers, token slips, patient files, billing notes, diagnostic orders, and pharmacy handoffs. A restaurant moves between table requests, kitchen tickets, staff memory, billing tools, and owner spreadsheets. A team loses decisions between chat threads, task boards, files, meetings, and personal reminders.

Who owns this? What changed? What happens next? Where is the latest information? Can the right person see the right thing at the right time?

Nexus builds software for those moments. We turn fragmented workflows into clearer systems.

How we think about software

Every useful workflow has roles, states, handoffs, and trust boundaries. Most software is described as a list of features. We start somewhere else.

We ask: Who is involved? What does each person need to know? What state is the work in? What action should happen next? What should be visible, hidden, confirmed, reviewed, or recorded?

A receptionist does not need the same screen as a doctor. A kitchen does not need the same screen as a restaurant owner. A developer does not need a vague security report. The right software gives every role a clearer view of the same journey.

The founder

Nexus is built and run by one founder — not a large agency, not a studio with account managers between you and the build. I came from operational contexts before engineering ones — clinics, kitchens, security audits, and early-stage startups — which is why the software above is organized around roles and handoffs instead of feature lists.

Every case study in this reference was scoped, designed, and built by me end to end. For larger engagements or tighter timelines, I bring in trusted contractors for additional capacity — but I stay hands-on through architecture and delivery on every project. If you work with Nexus, you're working directly with the person who understands your system, not someone relaying to a team you'll never talk to.

We don't hide our limits

We do not believe software should hide its limits. Health products should not pretend to diagnose. Safety products should not guarantee safety. Security tools should not promise total protection.

Trust begins with useful software — and honest communication about what it does.

SafeDate

The Problem

Modern dating often starts digitally, but the safety planning around meeting someone is still informal. A friend may know part of the plan. A location may be shared in a message. A check-in may be forgotten. The loop may never be closed.

Safety planning should feel like preparation, not alarm.

How check-ins work

When you create a date plan in SafeDate, you set a check-in time — typically the end of the date. At that time, SafeDate sends you a prompt. If you confirm you're safe, your trusted contacts see a green status and nothing else happens. If you don't respond within a configurable grace period (5–30 minutes, your choice), your trusted contacts receive an alert with your last shared plan details. Trusted contacts do not need a SafeDate account to receive alerts — they're reached by SMS or email. No location tracking occurs unless you choose to share it.

Integrations & Notifications

SafeDate reaches trusted contacts via SMS and email — no app install required on their end. Push notifications are available for SafeDate users on mobile. The product does not integrate with phone emergency services directly; if you believe you are in danger, contact emergency services first.

Disclaimer: SafeDate cannot guarantee personal safety, predict another person's behavior, prevent harm, or replace emergency services. Users should contact appropriate emergency support when they believe they are in danger.

Product Principles

  • Private execution first — work privately, share selectively.
  • Selective public updates — the founder controls what becomes public.
  • Milestone storytelling — progress organized around what was built.
  • Consistent publishing rhythm — without requiring oversharing.
  • No forced transparency — the private/public boundary stays in the founder's control.

What makes it different from posting on X

X (Twitter) threads are ephemeral and algorithm-dependent. A BuildPublic project page is permanent, structured, and owned by you — it lives at your URL, not inside a platform that can change its algorithm or suspend your account. Your private task list stays private no matter what you choose to share publicly. You can share one milestone without exposing the ten tasks that led to it. The public log is also readable without an account, so anyone you send it to can follow your progress without signing up.

Integrations

BuildPublic public project pages generate an RSS feed — anyone can subscribe to your progress updates in any feed reader. Optional cross-posting to X and LinkedIn lets you push a public update to your existing audience with one click. GitHub integration is on the roadmap, allowing commits to automatically generate progress log entries.

Disclaimer: BuildPublic does not guarantee audience growth, customer acquisition, revenue, funding, product success, launch performance, or social-platform reach. Users should avoid publishing confidential information, personal data, customer data, credentials, or unapproved claims. Public sharing should remain intentional and controlled.
